01 / About

Scholarship at the
intersection of affect and power

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Bilkent University's Department of International Relations, working on the Carnegie Corporation-funded Great Realignment: Small-State Statecraft and the Global Order project alongside Assoc. Prof. Efe Tokdemir. I also serve as a Visiting Lecturer, currently teaching IR 236: 20th Century World Politics.

My research sits at the intersection of political psychology, critical security studies, and foreign policy analysis. I investigate how emotions, ontological security needs, and identity narratives inform state behaviour — with a particular focus on Turkey's evolving relationship with the European Union and the West under the AKP era.

My work has appeared in journals including Third World Quarterly, Journal of European Integration, Turkish Studies, Critical Studies on Security, and Globalizations, among others. I hold a PhD from Kadir Has University (2025), an MA from Sabancı University, and a BA from Istanbul Bilgi University.
